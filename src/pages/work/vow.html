Background

In-housing digital advertising

Vow enables brands to take back control of their marketing supply chain.

Increasingly brands are bringing marketing in-house, and they need new solutions to effectively replace what they have relied upon external agencies to provide for them.

No one knows their brand better than their own brand managers.

Vow was designed to be an easy to use solution that connects all of a customer's ad-tech stack and enables true omni-channel marketing.

Image collage showing various pages of the of VOW UI on desktop and mobile devices

Challenge

Smashing up the old boys' club

Media industries have evolved over time to do things in their own peculiar ways.

It is no mean feat to crack open the secretive world of the agencies, and democratise creative and media so that brands can learn to take things into their own hands.

Vow takes a streamlined, unified approach that takes the best of each and ditches the inefficient, the fraudulent, and the just plain ugly.

This approach poses a number of challenges: to retain learned behaviours while making precision improvements, all while making it as easy to use as possible.

Takeaways

Don't Repeat Yourself

For such a massive application in constant flux at the early startup stage, I had to be quite strategic in my approach to the design and prototype build.

For efficiency, not to mention my own sanity, I stuck to the DRY principle.

I used a heavily component-led design incorporating reusable Sketch symbols. This allowed rapid design updates whenever the need arose.

This approach was even more critical in the prototype, where a considerable data structure and reusable code components acted as the foundation behind the huge number of pages.

The outcome was a beautiful high-fidelity fully clickable prototype that the customer could present to potential partners and investors.
